NIT Patna (NITP) Course Curriculum Overview

The curriculum at National Institute of Technology Patna (NITP) is typically designed to build strong fundamentals, practical skills, and industry readiness. It generally includes core subjects, labs, electives, projects, and internship opportunities (depending on the program).

Common Structure (Most UG/PG Programs)

  • Foundation Courses: Mathematics, basic sciences, and introductory engineering topics.
  • Core Courses: Main subjects specific to the branch/discipline.
  • Laboratories: Practical experiments and hands-on sessions linked with core subjects.
  • Program Electives: Advanced subjects chosen based on interests (e.g., AI, VLSI, Power Systems).
  • Open Electives: Courses from other departments to broaden knowledge.
  • Projects: Mini projects and major/capstone projects (often in final year/semester).
  • Internship/Training: Industrial training or internship during breaks/semester (varies by program).
  • Humanities & Management: Communication skills, economics, entrepreneurship, and ethics.

Typical Semester-Wise Flow (UG)

  1. Sem 1–2: Basic sciences, math, programming basics, engineering fundamentals.
  2. Sem 3–4: Branch core starts, labs increase, foundational professional subjects.
  3. Sem 5–6: Advanced core, electives begin, projects/seminars start.
  4. Sem 7–8: Major project, specialized electives, internship/industry-oriented work.

Notes

Exact subjects and credits vary by program (B.Tech, M.Tech, MCA, PhD) and by department. For the most accurate and current curriculum, refer to the official NIT Patna department/course scheme provided on the institute website or academic handbook.
